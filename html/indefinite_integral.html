The indefinite integral of a function \( f(x) \) with respect to \( x \) is denoted by \( \int f(x) \, dx \). It represents the family of all antiderivatives of \( f(x) \). In other words, it is the set of all functions \( F(x) \) such that \( F'(x) = f(x) \).

Mathematically, we write:

\[ \int f(x) \, dx = F(x) + C \]

where \( F(x) \) is an antiderivative of \( f(x) \) and \( C \) is an arbitrary constant, known as the constant of integration.

The process of finding the indefinite integral is also called integration.
