Let \( \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n z^n \) be a complex power series that is absolutely convergent in the disk \( |z| < R \), where \( R \) is the radius of convergence. To prove that the series is uniformly convergent on any closed subdisk \( |z| \leq r < R \), we follow these steps:

1. Absolute Convergence Implies Bounded Terms: Since the series \( \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n z^n \) is absolutely convergent for \( |z| < R \), for any \( z \) in the subdisk \( |z| \leq r \), we have: \[ \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} |a_n z^n| \leq \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} |a_n| r^n. \] Since \( r < R \), the series \( \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} |a_n| r^n \) is convergent and its sum is finite.

2. Uniform Convergence Criterion: For \( \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n z^n \) to be uniformly convergent on \( |z| \leq r \), given any \( \epsilon > 0 \), there exists an \( N \) such that for all \( n > N \) and for all \( z \) with \( |z| \leq r \), \[ \left| \sum_{k=n}^{\infty} a_k z^k \right| < \epsilon. \] To prove this, note that: \[ \left| \sum_{k=n}^{\infty} a_k z^k \right| \leq \sum_{k=n}^{\infty} |a_k| |z|^k \leq \sum_{k=n}^{\infty} |a_k| r^k. \] Since \( \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} |a_k| r^k \) is convergent, for any \( \epsilon > 0 \), there exists an \( N \) such that: \[ \sum_{k=n}^{\infty} |a_k| r^k < \epsilon \quad \text{for all} \quad n > N. \] Therefore, \[ \left| \sum_{k=n}^{\infty} a_k z^k \right| \leq \sum_{k=n}^{\infty} |a_k| r^k < \epsilon \quad \text{for all} \quad n > N \quad \text{and} \quad |z| \leq r. \]

3. Conclusion: The power series \( \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n z^n \) is uniformly convergent on any closed subdisk \( |z| \leq r < R \). This follows from the fact that we can make the tail of the series as small as desired, uniformly for all \( z \) in the subdisk.
