Relationship Between Operations and Differentiation

Sum Rule

The derivative of the sum of two functions is the sum of the derivatives of the functions.

Let \( f(x) \) and \( g(x) \) be differentiable functions. Then:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} [f(x) + g(x)] = f'(x) + g'(x) \]

Proof:

Using the definition of the derivative, we have:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} [f(x) + g(x)] = \lim_{{h \to 0}} \frac{(f(x+h) + g(x+h)) - (f(x) + g(x))}{h} \]

Which simplifies to:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} [f(x) + g(x)] = \lim_{{h \to 0}} \left[ \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h} + \frac{g(x+h) - g(x)}{h} \right] \]

Since the limit of a sum is the sum of the limits:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} [f(x) + g(x)] = \lim_{{h \to 0}} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h} + \lim_{{h \to 0}} \frac{g(x+h) - g(x)}{h} \]

Thus, we get:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} [f(x) + g(x)] = f'(x) + g'(x) \]

Product Rule

The derivative of the product of two functions is given by:

Let \( f(x) \) and \( g(x) \) be differentiable functions. Then:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} [f(x) \cdot g(x)] = f(x) \cdot g'(x) + f'(x) \cdot g(x) \]

Proof:

Using the definition of the derivative, we have:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} [f(x) \cdot g(x)] = \lim_{{h \to 0}} \frac{f(x+h) \cdot g(x+h) - f(x) \cdot g(x)}{h} \]

Add and subtract \( f(x+h) \cdot g(x) \) inside the numerator:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} [f(x) \cdot g(x)] = \lim_{{h \to 0}} \frac{f(x+h) \cdot g(x+h) - f(x+h) \cdot g(x) + f(x+h) \cdot g(x) - f(x) \cdot g(x)}{h} \]

Factor out common terms:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} [f(x) \cdot g(x)] = \lim_{{h \to 0}} \left[ \frac{f(x+h) \cdot (g(x+h) - g(x))}{h} + \frac{g(x) \cdot (f(x+h) - f(x))}{h} \right] \]

Separate the limits:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} [f(x) \cdot g(x)] = \lim_{{h \to 0}} \frac{f(x+h) \cdot (g(x+h) - g(x))}{h} + \lim_{{h \to 0}} \frac{g(x) \cdot (f(x+h) - f(x))}{h} \]

Evaluate the limits:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} [f(x) \cdot g(x)] = f(x) \cdot g'(x) + f'(x) \cdot g(x) \]

Quotient Rule

The derivative of the quotient of two functions is given by:

Let \( f(x) \) and \( g(x) \) be differentiable functions, and \( g(x) \neq 0 \). Then:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} \left[ \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \right] = \frac{f'(x) \cdot g(x) - f(x) \cdot g'(x)}{[g(x)]^2} \]

Proof:

Using the definition of the derivative, we have:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} \left[ \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \right] = \lim_{{h \to 0}} \frac{\frac{f(x+h)}{g(x+h)} - \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}}{h} \]

Combine the fractions inside the limit:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} \left[ \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \right] = \lim_{{h \to 0}} \frac{f(x+h)g(x) - f(x)g(x+h)}{h \cdot g(x+h) \cdot g(x)} \]

Rewrite the numerator:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} \left[ \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \right] = \lim_{{h \to 0}} \frac{f(x+h)g(x) - f(x)g(x) + f(x)g(x) - f(x)g(x+h)}{h \cdot g(x+h) \cdot g(x)} \]

Factor out common terms:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} \left[ \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \right] = \lim_{{h \to 0}} \frac{g(x) \cdot (f(x+h) - f(x)) - f(x) \cdot (g(x+h) - g(x))}{h \cdot g(x+h) \cdot g(x)} \]

Separate the limits:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} \left[ \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \right] = \lim_{{h \to 0}} \frac{g(x) \cdot (f(x+h) - f(x))}{h \cdot g(x+h) \cdot g(x)} - \lim_{{h \to 0}} \frac{f(x) \cdot (g(x+h) - g(x))}{h \cdot g(x+h) \cdot g(x)} \]

Evaluate the limits:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} \left[ \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \right] = \frac {g(x) \cdot f'(x)}{[g(x)]^2} - \frac{f(x) \cdot g'(x)}{[g(x)]^2} \]

Combine the fractions:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} \left[ \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \right] = \frac{f'(x) \cdot g(x) - f(x) \cdot g'(x)}{[g(x)]^2} \]
