Definition of a Series

A series in mathematics is the sum of the terms of a sequence. Given a sequence of numbers \(a_1, a_2, a_3, \ldots\), the corresponding series is the expression:

\[ S = a_1 + a_2 + a_3 + \cdots \]

The partial sum of the first \(n\) terms of the series is denoted by \(S_n\) and is given by:

\[ S_n = \sum_{k=1}^n a_k = a_1 + a_2 + a_3 + \cdots + a_n \]

If the sequence of partial sums \(\{S_n\}\) converges to a limit \(S\) as \(n\) approaches infinity, the series is said to converge, and \(S\) is called the sum of the series. If the sequence of partial sums does not converge, the series is said to diverge.

Important Properties

1. Linearity

If \(\sum a_n\) and \(\sum b_n\) are convergent series, then for any constants \(c\) and \(d\), the series \(\sum (ca_n + db_n)\) is also convergent, and

\[ \sum (ca_n + db_n) = c \sum a_n + d \sum b_n \]

2. Comparison Test

If \(0 \leq a_n \leq b_n\) for all \(n\) and \(\sum b_n\) converges, then \(\sum a_n\) also converges. Conversely, if \(\sum a_n\) diverges, then \(\sum b_n\) also diverges.

3. Ratio Test

Consider a series \(\sum a_n\) with positive terms. Define:

\[ L = \lim_{n \to \infty} \left| \frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n} \right| \]

- If \(L < 1\), the series converges.
- If \(L > 1\) or \(L = \infty\), the series diverges.
- If \(L = 1\), the test is inconclusive.

Proof of Convergence for Geometric Series

A geometric series is a series of the form:

\[ \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} ar^n = a + ar + ar^2 + ar^3 + \cdots \]

where \(a\) is a constant and \(r\) is the common ratio.

Proof: To find the sum of the first \(n+1\) terms, consider the partial sum \(S_{n+1}\):

\[ S_{n+1} = a + ar + ar^2 + \cdots + ar^n \]

Multiply both sides of the equation by \(r\):

\[ rS_{n+1} = ar + ar^2 + ar^3 + \cdots + ar^{n+1} \]

Subtract the second equation from the first:

\[ S_{n+1} - rS_{n+1} = a - ar^{n+1} \]

Factor out \(S_{n+1}\) on the left side:

\[ S_{n+1}(1 - r) = a(1 - r^{n+1}) \]

Solving for \(S_{n+1}\), we get:

\[ S_{n+1} = \frac{a(1 - r^{n+1})}{1 - r} \]

If \(|r| < 1\), then as \(n \to \infty\), \(r^{n+1} \to 0\). Thus, the sum \(S\) of the infinite geometric series is:

\[ S = \lim_{n \to \infty} S_{n+1} = \frac{a(1 - 0)}{1 - r} = \frac{a}{1 - r} \]
