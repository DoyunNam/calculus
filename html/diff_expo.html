Differentiation of Exponential Functions

The exponential function is one of the most important functions in calculus, especially because of its unique properties under differentiation.

Differentiation of \( e^x \)

The function \( e^x \) is unique in that its derivative is itself. That is:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} e^x = e^x \]

Proof:

We start by considering the definition of the derivative:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} e^x = \lim_{{h \to 0}} \frac{e^{x+h} - e^x}{h} \]

Using the property of exponents, we can rewrite \( e^{x+h} \) as \( e^x \cdot e^h \):

\[ \frac{d}{dx} e^x = \lim_{{h \to 0}} \frac{e^x \cdot e^h - e^x}{h} \]

Factoring out \( e^x \) from the numerator gives:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} e^x = \lim_{{h \to 0}} e^x \cdot \frac{e^h - 1}{h} \]

Since \( e^x \) is not dependent on \( h \), we can move it outside of the limit:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} e^x = e^x \cdot \lim_{{h \to 0}} \frac{e^h - 1}{h} \]

It is a well-known limit that:

\[ \lim_{{h \to 0}} \frac{e^h - 1}{h} = 1 \]

Thus, we have:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} e^x = e^x \cdot 1 = e^x \]

Differentiation of \( a^x \) (where \( a \) is a constant)

For a general exponential function \( a^x \), the derivative is given by:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} a^x = a^x \ln(a) \]

Proof:

We start with the definition of the derivative:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} a^x = \lim_{{h \to 0}} \frac{a^{x+h} - a^x}{h} \]

Using the property of exponents, we can rewrite \( a^{x+h} \) as \( a^x \cdot a^h \):

\[ \frac{d}{dx} a^x = \lim_{{h \to 0}} \frac{a^x \cdot a^h - a^x}{h} \]

Factoring out \( a^x \) from the numerator gives:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} a^x = \lim_{{h \to 0}} a^x \cdot \frac{a^h - 1}{h} \]

Since \( a^x \) is not dependent on \( h \), we can move it outside of the limit:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} a^x = a^x \cdot \lim_{{h \to 0}} \frac{a^h - 1}{h} \]

We know from calculus that:

\[ \lim_{{h \to 0}} \frac{a^h - 1}{h} = \ln(a) \]

Thus, we have:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} a^x = a^x \cdot \ln(a) \]
