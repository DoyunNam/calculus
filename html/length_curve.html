Length of a Parametrized Curve

The length \( L \) of a parametrized curve defined by \( \mathbf{r}(t) = (x(t), y(t), z(t)) \) for \( t \) in the interval \([a, b]\) is given by the integral:

\[ L = \int_{a}^{b} \sqrt{\left( \frac{dx}{dt} \right)^2 + \left( \frac{dy}{dt} \right)^2 + \left( \frac{dz}{dt} \right)^2} \, dt \]

Examples

Example 1: Line Segment

Consider a line segment from \((0, 0)\) to \((1, 1)\) parametrized by \( \mathbf{r}(t) = (t, t) \) for \( t \) in \([0, 1]\).

Here, \(\frac{dx}{dt} = 1\) and \(\frac{dy}{dt} = 1\). The length \( L \) is:

\[ L = \int_{0}^{1} \sqrt{1^2 + 1^2} \, dt = \int_{0}^{1} \sqrt{2} \, dt = \sqrt{2} \cdot (1 - 0) = \sqrt{2} \]

Example 2: Circle

Consider a circle of radius \( r \) parametrized by \( \mathbf{r}(t) = (r \cos t, r \sin t) \) for \( t \) in \([0, 2\pi]\).

Here, \(\frac{dx}{dt} = -r \sin t\) and \(\frac{dy}{dt} = r \cos t\). The length \( L \) is:

\[ L = \int_{0}^{2\pi} \sqrt{(-r \sin t)^2 + (r \cos t)^2} \, dt = \int_{0}^{2\pi} \sqrt{r^2 (\sin^2 t + \cos^2 t)} \, dt = \int_{0}^{2\pi} r \, dt = r \cdot 2\pi = 2\pi r \]

Example 3: Helix

Consider a helix parametrized by \( \mathbf{r}(t) = (a \cos t, a \sin t, bt) \) for \( t \) in \([0, 2\pi]\).

Here, \(\frac{dx}{dt} = -a \sin t\), \(\frac{dy}{dt} = a \cos t\), and \(\frac{dz}{dt} = b\). The length \( L \) is:

\[ L = \int_{0}^{2\pi} \sqrt{(-a \sin t)^2 + (a \cos t)^2 + b^2} \, dt = \int_{0}^{2\pi} \sqrt{a^2 (\sin^2 t + \cos^2 t) + b^2} \, dt = \int_{0}^{2\pi} \sqrt{a^2 + b^2} \, dt = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2} \cdot 2\pi \]
