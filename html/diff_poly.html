Differentiation of \( x^r \)

The differentiation of \( x^r \) with respect to \( x \) is given by:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} x^r = r x^{r-1} \]

Proofs for Different Types of \( r \)

1. \( r \) is a Natural Number

Let \( r = n \) where \( n \in \mathbb{N} \). Using the definition of the derivative:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} x^n = \lim_{{h \to 0}} \frac{(x+h)^n - x^n}{h} \]

Using the binomial theorem, we get:

\[ (x+h)^n = \sum_{{k=0}}^{n} \binom{n}{k} x^{n-k} h^k = x^n + nx^{n-1}h + \frac{n(n-1)}{2} x^{n-2} h^2 + \cdots + h^n \]

So,

\[ \frac{(x+h)^n - x^n}{h} = nx^{n-1} + \frac{n(n-1)}{2} x^{n-2} h + \cdots + h^{n-1} \]

Taking the limit as \( h \to 0 \), all terms involving \( h \) vanish:

\[ \lim_{{h \to 0}} \frac{(x+h)^n - x^n}{h} = nx^{n-1} \]

2. \( r \) is an Integer

Let \( r = n \) where \( n \in \mathbb{Z} \). For negative integers, consider \( n = -m \) where \( m \in \mathbb{N} \). Then:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} x^{-m} = \frac{d}{dx} \left( \frac{1}{x^m} \right) \]

Using the chain rule:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} x^{-m} = -m x^{-m-1} \]

3. \( r \) is a Rational Number

Let \( r = \frac{p}{q} \) where \( p \in \mathbb{Z} \) and \( q \in \mathbb{N} \). We can use the chain rule by rewriting \( x^{\frac{p}{q}} \) as \( (x^{\frac{1}{q}})^p \):

\[ y = x^{\frac{1}{q}}, \quad \text{then} \quad y^q = x \quad \text{and} \quad y^{p} = x^{\frac{p}{q}} \]

Using the chain rule, we get:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} x^{\frac{p}{q}} = \frac{d}{dx} y^p = p y^{p-1} \cdot \frac{d}{dx} y = p x^{\frac{p}{q} - 1} \cdot \frac{1}{q} x^{\frac{1}{q} - 1} \]

Simplifying, we get:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} x^{\frac{p}{q}} = \frac{p}{q} x^{\frac{p}{q} - 1} \]

4. \( r \) is a Real Number

For real \( r \), we can use logarithmic differentiation. Let:

\[ y = x^r \quad \Rightarrow \quad \ln y = r \ln x \]

Differentiating both sides with respect to \( x \):

\[ \frac{1}{y} \frac{dy}{dx} = r \frac{1}{x} \]

Multiplying both sides by \( y = x^r \), we get:

\[ \frac{dy}{dx} = r x^{r-1} \]
