Radius of Convergence

The radius of convergence of a power series \[ \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n (z - z_0)^n \] is the distance from the center \( z_0 \) to the boundary within which the series converges. Formally, it is defined as the non-negative number \( R \) such that the series converges when \( |z - z_0| < R \) and diverges when \( |z - z_0| > R \).

Finding the Radius of Convergence

The radius of convergence can be found using the formula \[ \frac{1}{R} = \limsup_{n \to \infty} \sqrt[n]{|a_n|} \] or equivalently \[ R = \frac{1}{\limsup_{n \to \infty} \sqrt[n]{|a_n|}}. \]

Proof

Consider the power series \[ \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n (z - z_0)^n. \] We use the ratio test to determine the convergence of the series. Define \( L \) as \[ L = \lim_{n \to \infty} \left| \frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n} \right|. \]

The ratio test states that the series converges if \( L < 1 \) and diverges if \( L > 1 \). If \( L = 1 \), the test is inconclusive. To find the radius of convergence, consider \( |z - z_0| = r \): \[ \left| \frac{a_{n+1} (z - z_0)^{n+1}}{a_n (z - z_0)^n} \right| = \left| \frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n} \right| |z - z_0|. \] For convergence, we require \[ \left| \frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n} \right| |z - z_0| < 1. \] Thus, the series converges when \[ |z - z_0| < \frac{1}{L}. \]

Therefore, the radius of convergence \( R \) is given by \[ R = \frac{1}{L} = \frac{1}{\lim_{n \to \infty} \left| \frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n} \right|}. \] Alternatively, using the root test, we get \[ \frac{1}{R} = \limsup_{n \to \infty} \sqrt[n]{|a_n|}. \]

In conclusion, the radius of convergence \( R \) is \[ R = \frac{1}{\limsup_{n \to \infty} \sqrt[n]{|a_n|}}. \]
